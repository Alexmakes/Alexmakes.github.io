how a pen plotter works

A pen plotter moves its x-y platform by using a combination of stepper motors and belts, just like a 3D printer. However, instead of extruding plastic, a pen plotter holds a pen or other writing instrument that is moved across the surface of a piece of paper or other material. The platform is typically mounted on a set of linear bearings, which allow it to move smoothly and accurately in the x-y plane. As the motors turn, the belts pull the platform in the desired direction, allowing the plotter to create precise drawings and designs. By carefully controlling the motion of the platform and the pen, pen plotters can produce highly detailed and accurate artwork.

How to talk pen plotter

G-code is a programming language used to control the movements of pen plotters and other CNC machines. It consists of a series of commands that tell the plotter how to move the x-y platform and pen, when to turn the pen on and off, and at what speed to move.

In a pen plotter context, G-code is generated by a drawing program that creates a design or image, which is then converted into a series of G-code commands. These commands tell the plotter how to move the x-y platform and pen to recreate the design on a piece of paper or other material.

For example, a G-code command might look something like this: G1 X10 Y20 E0.3 F300. This command would tell the plotter to move the x-y platform to position X=10, Y=20 (measured in millimeters), while simultaneously lowering the pen to the paper and drawing a line with a thickness of 0.3mm at a speed of 300mm/min.
